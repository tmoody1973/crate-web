## Problem Statement

Crate is a first-of-its-kind AI music intelligence platform — not generative AI (Suno, Udio), but a research workspace that helps music professionals understand connections, history, and context. Spotify just launched SongDNA (March 24, 2026), validating the exact thesis Crate was built on. Spotify also acquired WhoSampled data to power SongDNA. Crate already does what SongDNA does and more — but nobody outside Radio Milwaukee knows it exists.

The goal is to create press visibility that positions Crate as the "deeper" version of what Spotify is now investing in, specifically to create acqui-hire conversations with Spotify, Apple Music, or music data companies.

## Demand Evidence

- Tarik uses Crate daily as a DJ and radio producer — built it for himself, can't stop using it
- Radio Milwaukee (88Nine, HYFIN, Rhythm Lab) uses Crate for daily show prep, news segments, and research
- Built in two weeks with Claude Code + Superpowers — demonstrates what a single builder can do with AI tooling
- Spotify's SongDNA launch and WhoSampled acquisition validate that major platforms want musical connection data

## Status Quo

- SongDNA launched March 24, 2026 — Premium-only, mobile-only, song-level connections only
- Spotify invested in WhoSampled specifically for sample/cover data
- No existing platform combines AI agent research + interactive components + connected services (Spotify, Slack, Google) for music professionals
- Generative AI music tools (Suno, Udio) get all the press — the "AI for understanding music" category is uncovered

## Recommended Approach

**A then C:** Press release + LinkedIn campaign first (creates public signal and "as seen in..." credibility), then direct outreach to acqui-hire targets with the demo video and press clips.

### Press release structure

**Headline:** "Milwaukee developer builds AI music intelligence platform that goes deeper than Spotify's new SongDNA — in two weeks"

**Sub-headline:** "Crate uses 20+ live data sources and Auth0 Token Vault to connect directly to users' Spotify, Slack, and Google accounts"

**Key narrative beats:**

1. **The hook:** Spotify launched SongDNA yesterday to show connections behind songs. A developer in Milwaukee already had this — and more.

2. **What Crate is:** An AI-powered music research workspace for DJs, radio producers, and crate diggers. Not generative AI — intelligence AI. It doesn't make music; it helps you understand music.

3. **What makes it different from SongDNA:**
   - Works for artists, genres, labels, and events — not just songs
   - Narrative storytelling with chapters, YouTube embeds, playable tracks
   - Cited sources from 20+ databases (not just Spotify's internal metadata)
   - Connected services: creates Spotify playlists, sends to Slack, saves to Google Docs
   - Influence mapping based on academic methodology (Badillo-Goicoechea 2025)

4. **The builder story:** Built in two weeks by Tarik Moody, a developer and DJ at Radio Milwaukee, using Claude Code. Demonstrates what a single person with AI tools can build.

5. **The technology:** Auth0 Token Vault for secure OAuth connections, OpenUI for dynamic component rendering, 20+ MCP tool servers for real-time data access.

6. **The validation:** Radio Milwaukee uses it daily for show prep across three stations (88Nine, HYFIN, Rhythm Lab).

7. **The bigger picture:** The "AI for music understanding" category is emerging. Spotify invested in WhoSampled. Crate is what happens when you give a music professional AI agent tools instead of just a chatbot.
